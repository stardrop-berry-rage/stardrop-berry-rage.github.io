General Representative

Tyne Jones

Running for: General Representative

Hey ANU! I’m Tyne, a first-year student studying International Relations and Political Science. I’m running to be a General Representative (Gen Rep) on ANUSA. I will fight for:

Fair and Flexible Assessment Support

  • EAP Certainty: End the stress of constantly reapplying for alternative assessments by integrating them into your EAP, so if it’s approved once, you’re covered for all courses.
  • Fair Appeals: If your ECA appeal is rejected, you shouldn’t be left with nothing. I’ll fight for the right to reapply for different provisions, so support will always match your needs.

Accessible and Reliable Mental Health Services

  • Grief Support: I’ll fight to secure accessible grief-specific services through external partnerships, so ANU can provide professional care whenever students need it.
  • Extend Priority Counselling: I’ll push to extend priority counselling to six weeks after a critical incident, ensuring students have stability and support throughout challenging periods.

I’m excited to be running with RAGE, a ticket that will deliver the services you need, build the campus you want, and fight for the education you deserve.

Strengthened Priority Counselling After Critical Incidents

  • Current Policy (SCTR 14.h): “Ensuring priority counselling is provided for impacted individuals in the first days and up to two weeks after the incident.”
  • Proposed Update: “Ensuring priority counselling is available and actively offered for impacted individuals in the first days and up to six weeks after the incident.”
  • Rationale:
    • Two weeks of access is insufficient for sustained support and recovery.
    • Six weeks matches ANU’s standard six-session counselling model, delivered in priority form (weekly instead of fortnightly).
    • Provides continuity of care at a vulnerable time.

Grief Counselling Provision

  • Current Situation:
    • No specialist grief counsellor employed at ANU Counselling.
    • No formal partnership with an external grief counselling provider.
  • Proposed Update: Establish a partnership with an external specialist grief counselling provider.
  • Outcome:
    • Students can access grief-specific support at no cost.
    • Ensures no student is turned away during times of grief.

Educational Access Plan (EAP) Reform

  • Current Process:
    • Students must submit a Health Report to obtain an EAP.
    • Health Report includes “Recommendations for Reasonable Adjustments,” but does not specifically accommodate alternative assessment methods (e.g. “no exams," “take-home assessment”).
    • Students requiring alternatives must apply separately to the Exams Office each time.
      • For a 4-year full-time degree with two alternative assessments per course per semester → 64 separate applications.
  • Proposed Update:
    • Amend Health Report & EAP system to include Alternative Assessment Methods in the EAP.
    • Practitioner specifies:
      • What is being alternated from → to.
      • Reason(s) for recommendation.
    • Alternative assessment requests included in the EAP → decisions on what form it takes automatically delegated to course convenor (with student and possible Exams Office consultation/input).
  • Outcome:
    • Reduces administrative burden and stress on students.
    • Aligns adjustments with health practitioner recommendations.
    • Provides consistency/certainty in support across a student’s degree.

Educational Access Plan (EAP) Reform

  • Current Process:
    • Students may apply for one provision only (e.g. extension, changed weighting, or alternative assessment).
    • If rejected, they may appeal but cannot apply for a different provision.
    • Result: Some students are left with no approved support if their appeal is rejected, even if their health practitioner recommended an alternative.
  • Proposed Update:
    • Students may still apply for only one provision at a time and retain right of appeal.
    • If both the initial application and appeal are rejected, students may then apply for a different provision under a new ECA.
  • Outcome:
    • Ensures students are not left without options for support by the Exams Office.
    • Maintains one-provision-at-a-time principle but adds flexibility.
    • Balances student wellbeing with manageable administrative processes.
